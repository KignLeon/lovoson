# 🔥 Lovoson Media — Cold Call Script
## Maximum Appointment-Booking Framework

> **Role:** Phone Setter  
> **Goal:** Book a Discovery Call  
> **Pipeline Stage Flow:** Cold Prospect → Engaged → Warm / Qualified → Appointment Booked  
> **Average Call Time:** 3–5 minutes (never exceed 7)

---

## Pre-Call Prep (30 seconds)

Before dialing, have answers to:

| Question | Where to find it |
|---|---|
| What's their business name? | CRM `business_name` field or lead list |
| What niche are they in? | Google search / Yelp / their website |
| Do they have a website? | Quick search — note quality |
| How are their Google reviews? | Google Maps — note count + rating |
| Any obvious gaps? | No online booking? Slow website? Bad reviews? Few reviews? |

> **IMPORTANT:** The #1 predictor of cold call success is **relevance**. The 30 seconds you spend here is the difference between "not interested" and "tell me more."

---

## PHASE 1: The Opener (0–15 seconds)

### Primary Opener — "Honest Disarm"

```
"Hey [FIRST NAME], this is Leon from Lovoson Media — 
I know this is a cold call, I'll be honest with you. 
Do you have 30 seconds so I can tell you why I'm reaching out, 
and if it doesn't make sense, you can hang up on me?"
```

### Why this works:
- **Pattern interrupt** — acknowledging it's a cold call disarms the reflex to hang up
- **Honesty frame** — builds instant micro-trust
- **Low commitment ask** — "30 seconds" feels risk-free
- **Permission-based** — giving them control paradoxically keeps them on the line

### If they say "I'm busy":
```
"Totally get it. I'll be super quick — 
is there a better time later today or tomorrow I can call back for 2 minutes?"
```
→ If they give a time: book callback, tag `status:engaged`, add Setter Note  
→ If they say no: "No worries at all, have a great day [NAME]."

### If they say "Who is this?" / "What's this about?":
```
"Good question — I work with [NICHE] businesses in [CITY/STATE] 
and we help them fill their schedule using AI-powered follow-up systems. 
I had a quick question about your business, mind if I ask?"
```

---

## PHASE 2: The Permission-Based Hook (15–45 seconds)

> **TIP:** Your only job here is to get them **talking about their problems**. Ask ONE of these based on what you learned in pre-call research.

### Option A — Review/Reputation Angle (if they have < 50 Google reviews or < 4.5 stars)
```
"I was actually looking at your Google profile before this call — 
you've got [X] reviews, which is solid. But I'm curious — 
do you have anything in place to automatically ask customers for reviews 
after a job, or is that kind of falling through the cracks?"
```

### Option B — Lead Follow-Up Angle (works for ALL niches)
```
"Quick question for you — when a new lead comes in, whether it's from 
your website, Google, a referral, whatever — how fast are you or your team 
getting back to them right now?"
```

### Option C — Booking/No-Show Angle (service businesses)
```
"Out of curiosity — when someone calls to book with you guys and you miss 
the call, what happens? Do you have something that automatically texts 
them back, or does that lead just kind of disappear?"
```

### Option D — Website/Online Presence (if their site is weak or nonexistent)
```
"I was on your website before this call — are you happy with how it 
represents your business, or is that something that's been on the 
back burner?"
```

> **Read the room.** Pick the angle where their GAP is most obvious. If you found it in pre-call research, use it. If not, Option B (lead follow-up speed) works universally.

---

## PHASE 3: Pain Discovery — SPIN Lite (45 seconds – 2 minutes)

Once they start talking, **shut up and listen.** Then go deeper with ONE follow-up from each level:

### Situation (understand current state)
```
"Got it — so right now, [RESTATE WHAT THEY SAID]. 
How long has it been like that?"
```

### Problem (surface the pain)
```
"And when leads fall through the cracks like that — 
what does that actually cost you? Like, rough ballpark, 
how much is a new [customer/patient/client] worth to you?"
```

### Implication (amplify the cost)
```
"So if you're losing even [2-3] of those a month, 
that's [QUICK MATH] in revenue just walking out the door. 
And I'm guessing that adds up over a year, right?"
```

### Need-Payoff (get THEM to state the solution)
```
"If you could snap your fingers and have every lead followed up 
within 60 seconds — automatically — missed calls texted back, 
reviews collected on autopilot — would that move the needle for you?"
```

> **CAUTION: NEVER pitch before Phase 3 is complete.** If you haven't heard them describe their pain in their own words, you haven't earned the right to bridge to the offer. Stay in discovery.

---

## PHASE 4: Offer Bridge (30 seconds)

> Only proceed here if they've confirmed "yes" or showed clear interest in the need-payoff question.

```
"Cool — so here's why I called. We've built an AI-powered system 
specifically for [NICHE] businesses that does exactly that. 

It follows up with every lead in under 60 seconds — by text, email, 
even AI voice — so you never lose another customer to slow follow-up.

We also automate review collection, missed call text-backs, 
and appointment reminders so your no-shows drop and your 
Google profile grows on autopilot.

The best part? We set the whole thing up for you — 
you don't have to touch it."
```

> Keep it **tight**. No feature-dumping. Three outcomes they care about:
> 1. Never lose leads to slow follow-up
> 2. More Google reviews without asking
> 3. Fewer no-shows, more booked appointments

---

## PHASE 5: The Close — Book the Discovery Call (30 seconds)

### Primary Close — Assumptive
```
"What I'd love to do is get you on a quick 15-minute call 
with our team. We'll actually pull up your online presence live, 
show you exactly where you're leaving money on the table, 
and walk you through how the system works — no pitch, no pressure. 

Does [DAY] at [TIME] or [DAY] at [TIME] work better for you?"
```

### Why this works:
- **"15 minutes"** — low time commitment
- **"Pull up your online presence live"** — curiosity + value in the call itself
- **"No pitch, no pressure"** — lowers resistance
- **Two-option close** — "which" not "if"

### If they say yes:
```
"Perfect — I'm sending you a calendar confirmation right now. 
You'll get a text and email with the details. 
Can I grab your best email to send that over?"
```
→ Book in GHL calendar  
→ Tag: `status:appointment-booked`  
→ Move opportunity to: **Appointment Booked**  
→ Fill in: `primary_pain_point`, `setter_notes`, `lead_temperature`

### If they say "just send me info":
```
"Happy to — and honestly, the best info I can give you 
is a live look at your business in our system. 
It's way more valuable than a PDF. 
That's what the 15-minute call is — 
would [DAY] afternoon or [DAY] morning work?"
```

## ❌ Objection Handling Playbook

### "I'm not interested."
```
"Totally fair — most people aren't until they see what they're 
actually losing. Out of curiosity, do you know how fast 
your team responds to new leads right now?"
```
→ If they engage → loop back to Phase 2  
→ If they double-down → "No problem at all. Have a great day, [NAME]."

### "How much does it cost?"
```
"Great question — it depends on which package makes sense for you. 
Our clients typically invest somewhere between $999 and $2,000 
to get set up, and it pays for itself within the first month 
from leads you'd have otherwise lost. 

That's actually what the 15-minute call covers — 
we figure out which option fits your business. 
Does [DAY] work for a quick call?"
```

> **WARNING: NEVER deep-dive pricing on a cold call.** The goal is the appointment, not the sale. If they push hard on price, give the range and immediately redirect to the booking.

### "I already have a marketing guy / agency."
```
"Nice — that's actually a good sign, it means you take marketing 
seriously. Quick question though — does your current setup 
automatically follow up with leads in under 60 seconds? 
And does it handle missed call text-backs and review automation?

Because that's really where we live — we're not replacing your 
marketing, we're making sure the leads they generate 
actually turn into booked appointments."
```

### "I need to think about it."
```
"Of course — and honestly, the call IS the thinking-about-it step. 
It's 15 minutes where we show you exactly what the system does 
for businesses like yours. No commitment after that. 
Would [DAY] work to at least take a look?"
```

### "Can you call me back later?"
```
"Absolutely — when's a good time? I want to make sure I catch you 
when you've got a minute."
```
→ Book callback in CRM  
→ Tag: `status:engaged`  
→ Set task reminder

### "I don't have the budget."
```
"I hear you — and budget is always a real consideration. 
The reason most of our clients say it pays for itself is 
because they were already losing way more than [PRICE] in 
leads that never got followed up. 

I'm not asking you to commit to anything — just take a look 
on a 15-minute call and decide if the math makes sense. Fair?"
```

### "Just email me."
```
"I can do that — but honestly, a generic email won't do it justice. 
What makes our call valuable is I'll pull up YOUR actual business, 
YOUR Google profile, YOUR response time — and show you exactly 
where the gaps are. That's worth 15 minutes, right? 
How's [DAY]?"
```

## Post-Call CRM Workflow

Immediately after every call, update the following:

| Action | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Pipeline Stage** | Move to appropriate stage (Engaged / Warm / Appointment Booked / Disqualified) |
| **Lead Temperature** | Hot / Warm / Lukewarm / Cold |
| **Primary Pain Point** | In their words — what problem resonated most |
| **Setter Notes** | Key details for the closer (tools used, objections raised, personality notes) |
| **Tags** | Apply relevant tags: `status:engaged`, `setter:phone-assigned`, etc. |
| **Callback** | If promised, create task with exact time |

---

## Disqualification Criteria

Hang up gracefully and tag appropriately if:

| Signal | Tag |
|---|---|
| Revenue under $10K/month (can't afford either package) | `dq:no-revenue` |
| Explicitly states zero budget and no timeline | `dq:no-budget` |
| Hostile, rude, or abusive | `dq:conduct` |
| Already using a competitor's system AND happy with it | `dq:competitor` |
| Wrong contact / business not a fit | `dq:unresponsive-3x` (after 3 attempts) |

## 🧠 Mindset Rules

1. **You are not selling.** You are qualifying and booking. The closer sells.
2. **Tonality > words.** Say every line like you're talking to a friend, not reading a script. Calm, curious, confident.
3. **Silence is your weapon.** After asking a question, shut up. Let them fill the gap. The prospect who is talking is the prospect who is booking.
4. **Detach from outcome.** Not every call books. Your job is reps. Volume + quality = appointments.
5. **First 5 seconds decide everything.** If your opener sounds scripted, robotic, or salesy — they hang up before you finish. Practice the opener until it sounds like a normal human sentence.
6. **Always end with respect.** Every "no" today is a potential "yes" in 6 months. Never burn a bridge. Tag them `reactivation` and move on.

```
                  ↓

                  ↓

                  ↓

                  ↓

```

**Total call time: 3–5 minutes.**
